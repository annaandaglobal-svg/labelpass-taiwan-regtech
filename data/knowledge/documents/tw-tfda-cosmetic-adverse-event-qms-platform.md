# TFDA Post-Market Quality Management System

Source: https://qms.fda.gov.tw/

## Extract

Official TFDA post-market quality management portal for cosmetics and related regulated products. Use this source when a Taiwan cosmetics review needs to confirm adverse-event reporting route, post-market surveillance workflow, defective product handling, recall linkage, serious adverse effect escalation, complaint handling, or responsible-firm post-market obligations. The portal is operational and may require interactive browser access, account login, or form navigation, so LabelPass stores this structured operational extract as the reusable evidence anchor while retaining the live official URL for verification. Pair it with TFDA adverse-event reporting guidance and the MOJ serious adverse reporting regulation before making a final dossier decision. Practical review workflow: identify the product name, product notification or registration context, responsible firm, importer, manufacturer, lot or batch number, complaint date, event date, event narrative, injury or hygiene/safety hazard description, corrective action, recall or withdrawal status, reporter contact, follow-up record, and whether the event meets serious adverse effect reporting thresholds. Search and routing aliases include QMS, post-market quality management, adverse event, serious adverse effect, cosmetics complaint, defective cosmetic, recall, product safety complaint, 衛生安全危害, 嚴重不良反應, 化粧品不良事件, 產品回收, 품질관리, 이상사례, 부작용, 회수, 사후관리, and 대만 화장품 안전 보고. In label or claim screening, flag this source when files mention consumer injury, irritation, allergy, infection, product defect, contamination, microbial issue, safety complaint, recall trigger, or medical consultation. If the portal cannot be reached automatically, record the attempted search keyword, account or form route, visible screenshot, and companion legal source used. If product identity, lot, responsible firm, or event severity cannot be matched, route the review to expert follow-up and do not treat marketing safety claims as substantiated.
