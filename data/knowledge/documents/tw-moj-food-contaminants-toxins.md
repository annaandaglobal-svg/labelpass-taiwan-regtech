Article 2 Contaminants as referred to these standards shall mean the substance not intentionally added to food, which present in food as a result of production or pollution during the manufacturing, processing, preparing, packaging, transportation, storage, or selling, or as a result of environmental contamination, but do not include insect fragments, hairs and other extraneous foreign matters. Toxins as referred to these standards including the mycotoxins, marine bio-toxins and natural toxins in plant origin. The scope of these standards does not include the residues of pesticides, veterinary drugs and processing aids; the tolerance of atomic dust and radioactivity contamination, polychlorinated biphenyl and dioxins; and the microorganisms and microbial toxins that are within the Sanitation Standards for Microorganisms in Foods. Article 3 The maximum levels of metals in foods shall meet the standards in Annex table 1.
